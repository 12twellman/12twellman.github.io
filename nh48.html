The New Hampshire 4000 Footers

New Hampshire is home to some of the tallest mountains in the Appalacian range and 48 of their peaks rise over 4000 feet tall. These mountains are part of the "New Hampshire 48-4000 Footers List." This list is composed of all the mountains that meet the qualifications of rising above sea level beyond 4000 feet. Many New England locals attempt to climb all 48 mountains on the list. It is a prestigious hiking goal, started by many and accomplished by few.

Mount Washington

The top 5 tallest mountains in New Hampshire are: Mt. Washington, Mt. Adams, Mt. Jefferson, Mt. Monroe, and Mt. Madison.

The best way to approach this list, is to start from some of the easier mountains and build your strength up for the taller ones. This is the recommended start order based on difficulty level:

Mount Osceola: It has the least amount of elevation gain and it's all spread over 3 miles. This makes for an easy hike, and it has great views. Trailhead information can be found here

Mount Tecumseh: This mountain has a small elevation gain, but it is a shorter hike so it can be a bit steeper. The views at the top are also very impressive as they look out over waterville valley. Trailhead information can be found here

Mount Hale: This is a shorter but steeper hike. The view from the summit is not visible due to the trees, but many views are available while hiking that make the trip worth it. Trailhead information can be found here

Mount Jackson: This is a shorter hike to the summit and it has amazing views. It has a few steep sections, but is generally considered a very easy hike. Trailhead information can be found here

Mount Pierce: Another trail that is gradually graded and easy to hike. The views from the top overlooking Crawford Notch are spectacular. Trailhead information can be found here
